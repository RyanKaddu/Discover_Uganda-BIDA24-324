About Uganda

Discover the rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty of the Pearl of Africa

The Pearl of Africa

Uganda, often referred to as the "Pearl of Africa" by Winston Churchill, is a landlocked country in East Africa known for its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and warm, welcoming people.

With a population of over 45 million people from more than 56 indigenous communities, Uganda is a melting pot of cultures, languages, and traditions.

The country is blessed with an abundance of natural wonders, including the source of the Nile River, snow-capped mountains, vast lakes, and dense rainforests that are home to half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas.

Quick Facts

Location

East Africa, bordered by Kenya, South Sudan, DR Congo, Rwanda, and Tanzania

Geography

Diverse landscapes from savannahs to mountains, lakes, and rainforests

Languages

English (official), Swahili, Luganda, and over 50 local languages

Climate

Tropical climate with average temperatures of 25-29°C (77-84°F)
